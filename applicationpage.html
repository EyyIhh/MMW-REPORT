Applications of Linear Inequalities

Budgeting and Saving Money

Linear inequalities help you plan your spending and avoid overspending. For example, if you want to spend less than $100 on school supplies, you can write: x < 100, where x is the total cost.

Why is this useful? It helps you set a limit and make sure you do not exceed your budget. If you buy notebooks for $20 each and pens for $10 each, you can write an inequality: 20n + 10p < 100 to find out how many items you can buy.

Scenario: You want to buy snacks for a party, but you only have 500 pesos. If chips cost 50 pesos per pack and drinks cost 30 pesos per bottle, you can use the inequality 50c + 30d ≤ 500 to plan your purchases.

Cooking and Recipes

Recipes often use inequalities to describe ingredient limits. For example, "use less than 2 teaspoons of salt" can be written as s < 2.

Why is this useful? It helps you avoid using too much of an ingredient, which could ruin the taste or make the food unhealthy.

Scenario: A recipe says you can use up to 500 grams of flour. If you already used 300 grams, the inequality f + 300 \leq 500 tells you how much more flour you can add.

Travel and Distance

If you want to travel no more than 50 kilometers in a day, you can write: d ≤ 50, where d is the distance traveled.

Why is this useful? It helps you plan your route and manage your time, especially if you have limited fuel or time.

Scenario: You are planning a bike trip and want to ride less than 30 kilometers before lunch. If you have already traveled 18 kilometers, the inequality 18 + x < 30 tells you how much farther you can go.

School Grading

To pass a subject, you may need a grade of at least 75. This can be written as g ≥ 75, where g is your grade.

Why is this useful? It helps you set goals and track your progress. You can use inequalities to know how much you need to score on the next test to pass.

Scenario: If your current average is 70 and you have one more test worth 30 points, you can use the inequality (70 + t)/2 ≥ 75 to find the minimum score t you need.

Practice Problem

You want to buy notebooks that cost 15 pesos each. You have at most 100 pesos. Write and solve an inequality to find how many notebooks you can buy.

Let n be the number of notebooks: 15n ≤ 100.
Solution: n ≤ 6 (You can buy up to 6 notebooks).

Why is this useful? It helps you make decisions and avoid spending more than you have.

Scenario: If you also want to buy a pen for 10 pesos, the inequality becomes 15n + 10 ≤ 100. Now, n ≤ 6 (since 15*6 + 10 = 100).
